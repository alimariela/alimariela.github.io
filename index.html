Living the high life

August 27, 2019

The first day of my most recent promotion to Visual Manager extraodnaire of a multi-million dollar store. A store that had been pegged as the epitome of future growth and targeted for a big renovation under my watch.

It didn't all start here of course. Recently, having celebrated my 14th year with the company, I had worked my way up.

YEAR STORE POSITION
2005 Natick Staff
2006 Boston Staff
2007 Natick Assist. Visual Manager
2010 Boston AVM
2014 Cambridge Visual Manager
2019 Natick VM

However, it ended where it all began. Having started my career at the same store where it ended was nothing short of a career full circle. I started as staff and came back as HBIC.

2020

April 167, 2020

I went in fully loaded and ready to party. After, a successful execution of Christmas and fourth quarter, I treated myself and family to a fabulous vacation before I became locked down at work with renovation projects.

And "lock down" we did...just not how I originally thought it would happen. My family and I returned from our Aruba vacation on March 9th. By March 17th, I was furloughed and virtually schooling my little one.

I was called to return to work by early June. By the end of June, I was laid off. Any and all capital projects, including renovations were cancelled until further notice and my job ceased to exist. I was one of the lucky ones.

Current status

present day

I am lucky enough to have a wonderful husband who can work from home and brings home the bacon! I was called back to help with holiday execution. I went back part-time as I am now part-time student, part-time stay at home mom, part-time worker bee.

I'm proud of my accomplishments in my previous life as a retail executive, however, I can see the writing on the wall for brick and mortar retail. It's time for me to move up and out. What better way to start a new career than to learn something completely different, new, and something I never ever ever thought I could do!! Life is good!
